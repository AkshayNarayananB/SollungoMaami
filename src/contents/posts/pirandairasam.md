### **Make Pirandai Rasam Podi**
1. Apply oil to hands, clean pirandai, remove sharp edges & fibres, and chop into small pieces.  
2. Dry roast **toor dal, coriander seeds, black pepper, red chillies** on medium-low flame until dal turns golden brown; transfer to plate.  
3. Dry roast **curry leaves** until crispy and add to the roasted mix.  
4. Dry roast **pirandai** on low flame until the raw smell disappears and it turns slightly crisp; cool completely.  
5. Add **cumin seeds (raw)** and generous hing powder to the cooled mixture.  
6. Grind all ingredients to a **slightly coarse powder** and store in an airtight container.

### **Make Pirandai Rasam**
1. Heat **2 tbsp ghee** in a pot.  
2. Add **mustard seeds**; allow them to splutter.  
3. Add **finely chopped tomatoes** and cook until soft and mushy.  
4. Add **turmeric powder and hing**.  
5. Add **3â€“4 tsp pirandai rasam podi**; sautÃ© briefly to bloom the spices.  
6. Add enough **water** based on desired rasam consistency. Boil for 8â€“10 minutes to infuse flavour.  
7. Add **salt**, keep flame low to medium-low, and add **generous coriander leaves**.  
8. Heat until rasam becomes **frothy/foamy on top**, then switch off. Cover to lock aroma.
